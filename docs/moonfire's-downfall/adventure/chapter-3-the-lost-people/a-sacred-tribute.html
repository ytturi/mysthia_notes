A sacred tribute
...

Upon visiting the shrine of the seven, the priest will introduce the party to the gods if they want to.

Din Silverleaf is upset because the orcs did break into the sacred forest and destroyed part of it without paying tribute to the goddess of the forest.

Read to players

Daelia, daughter of Xandir and Yferja, is the goddess of the forest and she maintains the balance in the forest.
It doesn't mean that people can't hunt animals or cut trees, but a tribute has to be payed for that.
For each life taken from the forest, she may take another from outside the forest.
Worst case scenario a lot of lives could be taken...

Usually a ritual is made every year to pay the tribute in advance, so the citizens have the freedom to do their work as usual until the following winter.
Due to the recent intrusion from the orcs and the Astraeran party the the balance was broken. I've tried talking to the local authorities but they don't mind about minor gods and disregard the matter. I've seen this happening many times and then they speak words of forgiveness for not acting before.

I need an emerald, but the cheapest one I could find was 50gp that the government refuses to pay. I could pay for it but that means that there will be a lack of medicines.

On the other hand, I'd be grateful if you could escort me to the heart of the forest and protect me while I pay the tribute to the goddess.

The party will exit Velien from the west road. Take a turn towards Iemin on the crossroad. Halfway there, Din will point towards the forest on a small trail almost invisible if you don't know it's there.

If any member of the party has passive perception 12 they will notice that upon entering the trail, the chirping of birds and other sounds of animals have stopped and the trail feels unusually calm.

Din will make sure that everyone sticks around, otherwise the characters will lose track of the group and eventually be back at the road with no clue where to go.

After some turns the trail stars to look like a cavity under the ground open from the top. It's narrow but there's enough space for two medium humanoids to stand in parallel. Define the party formation.

From this point, you can use the Daelia's forgotten shrine map, or use the zoomed images in this page.

After walking a bit more you encounter a clearance and a dead brown bear. Din hurries to check the body.

This is a protector of the forest! This isn't nice... There's no clear injury, it looks like some strange biting and ...

While he talks a group of giant spiders appear from the canopy. They were stealthily waiting in the trees.
Any character with a passive Wisdom (perception) of 12 will notice them and not be surprised. Characters actively looking for dangers must succeed a DC 13 Wisdom (perception)
During the second round of combat, more giant spiders will appear from the trail, both from the front and the back, as well as the sides.

In case of the spiders defeating someone they will keep them alive and try to take the body with them, retreating to the upper level in the process. Any captured character will be carried (alive) to the ironmouth cave

After the combat Din will warn the adventurers. This spiders are not common on this forest. Something is wrong and they have to hurry.
The path draws narrow ahead and now the party can move only in a one person line.

After a while, some wolves can be seen on the edges on top of the trail, in the upper level which is pretty high now (about 20ft) compared to the start of the trail.
When Din realizes, he searches his bag with the materials for the ritual and takes the emerald and holds it high for the wolves to see.

The wolves are protecting the sacred land and the smell of the spiders warned them of intruders. They were looking for them, but they do identify the pendant as a gift for the goddess.

The path will then open to a bigger clearance. This time it's a shrine. A seemingly round room with no exits but the one you come from.
A big white antler is close to a stone altar. There are some stone decorations that look old and mossy.

Din will make small bow and say: "Master Linael, I wasn't expecting you here!" and then explain to the party who he is.

The antler then will make some strange moves and transform into a humanoid version.

He is Linael Whispertree an elven druid that guards the forest. He is also an acolyte of Daelia, goddess of the forest.

He will be grateful for defeating the spiders but fears that they were mere scouts.

He also informs the party, even though he just refers to Din, that the forest is angry by the presence of the now gone orcs. Even though they are dead now, a tribute has yet to be paid or a wraith will raise.

After the warning he will transform again, into a goat this time. Climb the stone walls and get lost in the forest.

Din will share his worries with the party and then urge to start with the preparations.
The whole preparations take half an hour and the ritual itself takes one hour.
Din will bring some flowers, grain and seeds and create a magic circle using some of the reagents he brought in top of the altar.
For the last part he will put the emerald with a braided cord in the center of the cirle and chant some words in Sylvan.

Daelia's Sorrow: If someone understands sylvan they can understand the spell is like a song. A sad ballad about life, love and death which moral is about keeping bonds together and paying back what it was promised otherwise. Even after death.

After the ritual the pendant is left by the altar. Din is exahusted but says everything is done now.
Before leaving though, more giant spiders appear from the top of the clearance. They will try to get the pendant.
Also a giant constrictor snake will appear from the trail.
A second wave of spiders will appear when the first one reaches the ground. As it takes them a whole round to get to ground level.

Din asks for help as they are not children of the woods.
After the 4th round, if the spiders didn't steal the pendant, four wolfs will appear to help the party defeat the spiders.

Rewards
...

If the pendant is taken, Din will only award the party with one potion of healing as the pendant was stolen in front of them.
Daelia's blessing won't be applied either.

Din will reward the party with 4 potions of healing upon returning to the shrine.
Also all party members will recieve: Daelia's friendly blessing

Daelia's friendly blessing: For ten days beasts are innately friendly to you in the woodlands and the woodlands' animals won't attack you unless you attack first. Animal handling: +2.

Stealing the pendant
...

If the players decide to steal the pendant, it is no common pendant and after the ritual it has a blessing and a curse. You can find more in the Magic Items appendix: Daelia's pendant.
