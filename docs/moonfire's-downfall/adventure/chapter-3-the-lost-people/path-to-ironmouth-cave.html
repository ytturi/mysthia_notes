Mountain Path to the ironmouth cave
...

Once known, the adventurers can just follow the path and enter the mountain range.
There they must orient themselves to follow the steps told by the elders or use a map.

A careful approach will take about three days while a rushed approach would take them a day and a half. Otherwise the path will take about two days journey through the mountain range.
The path is easy to practice by foot. Even though it hasn't been maintained the path is clear while walking, unless they decide to wander around, they wouldn't get lost.
However, it is unusable for a cart or a Wagon. A mount that has been trained
to navigate through this terrain could be used, otherwise it is considered difficult terrain and they would move at walking speed most of the time.

Upon entering the mountain range, other dangers approach.
For each day spent in the mountain range you may find one random encounter. However, The manticore and the pegasus should happen only once.

On the way in I recommend Ettin on a bridge and Chained Ogre.
On the way back, Perytons and the Manticore and the pegasus.

Perytons
...

Two to three perytons (MM p251) attack from the sky trying to find a good meal.
They will attack and try to grab one creature and take it to their nest. Hopefully an elven-blood humanoid.

They are relentless and fight to the death.

Ettin on a bridge
...

An Ettin (MM p132) is resting under a broken stone bridge that follows the path. The creature will realize that someone is approaching and will demand payment for passage without specifying much.

One of the heads is interested in shinies, so it tries to collect all their shinies storing them in an old crate under the bridge. If discovered, it contains all types of shiny objects worth about 200gp, from random pieces of silver cutlery to coins of those who wandered through their path.

Actually it is interested in any kind of shining object, even if it's an illusion and has no real worth.

If found, the crate contains 50gp worth of coins (3d100cp 5d12sp 1d10gp), silver cutlery (1lb) worth 5gp, unmatching simple silver and bronze earrings and other jewelry (1lb) worth 100gp and two silver-coated daggers worth 50gp each.

The other head is interested in riddles and if the characters don't know how to pay or if they spend enough time discussing they will receive the exchange offer. One riddle for a riddle and they don't even ask for the answer of the riddle.

The ettin isn't very intelligent so they won't mind any sort of riddle, even if it doesn't make sense.

The ettin riddle

"One wanders into a dry..." The other head interrupts: "Shut up you are telling it wrong, that's not how it goes... The twinhead eats a big elk on the ohter side of the river, how is it?"

The ettin waits a bit then the first head continues: Wait that's not how it goes you missed the whole point. Let me start over: An ettin hates water and would kill his twinhead if it ever wants to go near it. The twinhead decides to cross a mountain creek to hunt an elk and they both eat meat peacefully on the other side. How could that be?"

The obvious answer is that the river is dry.

Resolving the situation peacefully allows the characters to ask the ettin for information. They have some:

  • Not many people wander these lands nowadays but those that do usually wear grey robes and follow the path to the scary red mouth of the mountain.
  • The people that cross always brings something new for us, be it shinies or riddles, in exchange we wait here for more.
  • Lately there are strange creatures around: big spiders and strange whispers are heard in the night.

Spider ambush
...

The characters reach a range of hills and woods with a small stream of water.
Characters with a passive Wisdom (perception) of 12 will notice that the canopy is slightly covered with spiderweb. Also noticing small almost invisible threads on the road. Once noticed they can easily be avoided.

Otherwise, unless the characters aren't moving carefully, allow them to make a Wisdom (perception) DC 12 or be surprised by the spiders' attack. A group of four giant spiders (MM p328) will ambush the group fighting to the death.

Chained Ogre
...

Travelling a bit further ahead there's a small patrol of four goblins (MM p.166) and a chained ogre. The Ogre is chained and the goblins are taking it towards the road.

This is easily noticed by the characters about 100ft before their paths collide. The mountain rocks and bushes as well as the general terrain model a good cover and disguise for the characters that are free to decide how to approach them.

If approached stealthfully, the goblins are talking about the rewards that their masters may give them. They are refering to the cult of Thanatos who have bought the goblins help in exchange for gold and the remains of their experiments.

Examples:

  • We have to bring you to the masters, they will give us nice rewards.
  • If we reach before noon we may even eat some nice human flesh

Once approached or after the characters attack, the ogre starts raging, grabbing the chains that bind them and trying to use them as weapons. Make a contested strength save each round to see if it breaks free. The goblins will make an attempt to subdue it, however, if the ogre is freed then they will start fleeing on random directions.

The ogre won't reason to anything however the goblins will rather flee or sell the little information that they have to survive. The goblins can also point out the general directions or escort the characters to the big red mouth cave.

What the goblins know

  • Their masters reside in the red cave that looks like a big mouth
  • Their masters give nice rewards as gold and human flesh in exchange for help in capturing big creatures like the ogre
  • Although they are interested in the rewards they are not allied to them and they don't know what is their purpose

The Manticore and the Pegasus
...

While on their way, the characters notice a one-in-a-lifetime event that not many have seen. A screech followed by deprecating messages in different languages is heard from quite a distance.

A two big creatures are fighting in the sky. A dragon-like winged creature and a feathered creature. They are actually a manticore (MM p213) and a pegasus(MM p250), however most of what the adventurers can tell is the details on the wings from this distance.

Suddenly they engage mid-air and fall to the ground on slightly different positions. Both of them will look rather wounded and try to convince the characters to help them. However, the pegasus doesn not speak so it will try to signal the characters who must succeed a DC 12 Intelligence (insight) to understand its intentions.

Both creatures nature are opposed so they are natural enemies. However, both are following their masters command to subdue the other creature and collect parts of it. The Manticore needs the pegasus heart and the pegasus needs the manticore's mane.

Both are hurt and prefer to take a short rest before pursuing their endeavors. If the characters wait escorting one of the creatures the scene will repeat once they take the short rest.

After the confrontation with the other creature, they will reward the characters with parts of their own bodies:

The manticore spines and part of its mane
The pegasus feathers

Both could be sold to an oddities collector for quite some gold (about 100gp each set of items) or maybe refined into alchemical components.

After being helped the remaining creature will thank the characters, in their own language. Animal speech for the pegasus, which will be understood if A sacred tribute was previously completed.

Thank you, [master] will be pleased.

Replacing it with the appropiate master:

  • The Manticore follows the spine, a being of great intelligence and power that is both to be admired and feared. While
  • The pegasus follows the lightbringer, the chosen of Yrthandir that wants to erradicate the evil to restore the balance in this lands.
