The lost infant of Velien - Part 1: Cart in the crossroads
...

Sheila mentioned that the merchants said the cart was near the crossroads between Goliath and Yluria, about four hours long following the road.
She doesn't have much but she is willing to use the winter's savings to find out what happened.

Although there are some locals that want to help, given the recent events noone wants to wander and risk their lives on the potential dangers of the road.

The crossroads
...

When reaching the crossroads the characters can easily spot the trail of the cart getting offroad with a passive perception of 12. Otherwise they will have to investigate the trails for 10 minutes.

Missing image

The trail just looks as if they entered the woods instead of following the road. The characters can see that some goods were taken. There's a clear trail through the woods towards the nearby mountain.

If they investigate the cart and it's surrondings:

SkillDCDiscovery
Intelligence (Investigation)10Looks like most of the goods were taken along with the people, but the tools were left behind. There are ropes and some wooden frames used to tighten the goods to the cart.
Wisdom (Survival)10There's a trail that leads to the nearby mountains.
Wisdom (Survival)12The footsteps indicate that at least 4 different adults and a child went towards the mountain.

Award 50XP for every clue that they can unveil.

The cave by the mountains
...

Following the trail leads to a small entrance into the mountains. This natural cave currently hosts some cultists of Thanatos. The cultists won't reveal their plans by common means but they are investigating the source of black magic and some ancient writtings (see 3rd room).

If the characters make noise inside the cave, the cultists will stay alert and start casting animate_dead in order to summon zombies and then they will escape through the upwards passage in the 3rd room.

Missing image

The entrance
...

In the entrance of the cave there are two traps. One of them is easily spotted by passive perception 10 or with a successful Wisdom (Perception) DC 8 when analyzing the room. Its a small thread knotted to a wooden spike. The character can follow it up to the ceiling, but the character won't be able to see the end without moving past the thread.

Before that trap, a well painted piece of parchment covers a 5ft wide hole in the ground. The trap explained before acts as a distraction. In order to detect this one a successfull Wisdom (Perception) DC 16 is required while actively looking for traps.

The traps are combined in a way that if the first one was not seen, after the intruder falls to the hole if the intruder tries to climb into the cave the second trap will be triggered.

TrapWisdom (Perception) DCPassive PerceptionFailure
Hole in the ground16-The character walks into the piece of parchment falling 6ft, taking 1d6 bludgeoning damage and being knocked prone.
The thread810The thread is tied to a refined mechanism that will drop some small boulders. Dealing 1d4 bludgeoning damage to the character below it. If the character is still in the hole, the damage is increased by another 1d4.

In order to avoid it, the characters can walk around the parchemnt and above the thread. Both can be easily avoided if one knows where they are. From the inside, the cultists are able to untie the thread and they also have a small wooden platform to cross easily without altering the parchment.

If the traps trigger, the noise will make the cultists in the following rooms stay alert. They will also signal the 6 skeletons that are hidden in the 1st room behind the stone walls to take care of the intruders. Then they will proceed with the escape plan.

The 1st room
...

If the traps weren't triggered as they enter the room, they will see the skeletons as the enter in their vision range. They look like creepy sculptures with rusty weaponry. They can be destroyed easily while in this state.

Destroying them will alert the cultists. You may roll initiative to help you track the time.

On the other hand if the skeletons aren't destroyed, they will activate once any unknown creature trespasses towards the inner side of the cavern (Passage to the 2nd room), in which case, the cultists are also alerted.

The 2nd room
...

If the party reaches the 2nd room unnoticed, there are 4 cultist of Thanatos (flesh). They will activate the skeletons (if they are not destroyed) when engaged in combat. The cultists are any kind of humanoid. They are commited to the cult to the point that they will fight to the death and if they are held prisioners, they will try to cast Thanato's sacrifice without a target in order to die for their cause without revealing information.

There are also 4 humanoid bodies laying in the room that the cultist would use as zombies if required.

In this room there are some boxes and barrels. They contain stolen goods required for their rituals as well as some dried food and kegs of water for them to live in this cave.

The goods for the ritual are rare goods like some sort of dried organs, tooths and bones, some dust-like substances and some plants.

The 3rd room
...

The lead ends in the 3rd room. There is noone in the third room. This small room looks more like a study and holds a wooden bed. It's easy to spot a backpack and a table with some books and papers lying around. Also the walls are covered with boxes and barrels.

Investigating the table the party will find some books and some notes. The books seem to be related to legends and tells of lesser gods involving inmortallity, some salms are left aside mentioning Thanathos. Some notes describe required materials, some are scratched and some aren't. There's also a mysterious letter from 'Elban' mentioning the infant, a ritual and that he is waiting in the Ironmouth cave. The adventurers will not know where that cave is.

Investigating the barrels and boxes they will discover that they are filled with materials for the rituals. One barrel contains human blood and another one contains some sort of murky water. However there is one barrel that catches the attention of the party that looks exactly as the orc's grog barrels, and indeed it contains orc grog.

With a successful Wisdom (Perception) or Intelligence (Investigation) DC 10 the party will find a small carving in the wall next to one bookcase in the table. Upon moving the bookcase there are two potions of healing and a small pouch with 150gp.

In this room there's also a backpack containing adventurer's gear: 10ft of rope, 4 candles, a bag of ball bearings, a bag of caltrops, and a climber's kit.

Returning to Sheila
...

Upon retrieving the information to Sheila, she will be grateful for the investigation success even though now she is more concerned about the well-being of her family.
She will mention that she remembers the elders of Yluria, where she was raised, talking about that cave when she was just a child. They may know where it's located.

Sheila will reward the adventurers with 100gp and will ask the adventurers to continue further with the investigation if they can.
