Moonfire's Manor
...

Introduction
...

Elwin will lead the adventurers into the suspected house that the cultists have occupied within the Argent highs.

The Moonfire family owns the house, so the manor is blue-colored following the family colors.

The manor is huge. We are talking hundreds of feet far and wide. While it is still within a city, a small garden surrounds the manor, beautifully decorated with trees, flower bushes, statues, and fountains. It seems like it's well-maintained.

From the outside, the top floors seem not to have any lights, while a few lights seem to be lit on the base floor.

If the characters are surveiling the building, by arriving earlier than the suggested midnight approach, they spot 7 different nobleman entering the building as if they were attending an event.

They stop at the entrance, and they all signal a secret knock (identified with a successful perception (Widsom) DC 15.

Entering the Manor
...

Servants should answer the door if knocked, however, they are reluctant to let anyone through without a valid reason.

Some reasons the servants may use:

  • We have not heard of your inquiry before, please send a letter and we'll inform you of the best time to come back.
  • The lord of the house is not currently available, please come at another time.
  • There's a private event tonight, only members with invitation may enter.

Failing to persuade or coerce the servants makes them shut the door and remain vigilant. If there is further inquiry then they trigger the security guards (statues and gargoyles).

If the conversation is not successful, Elwin will cast Charm Person as a last resort and use it to gain access to the building as well as a guide.

Manor interior
...

  • Walls. Each floor of the manor is 15ft high, enough to fit a small giant. Even the tiniest of the corridors is at least 10ft wide. All the walls seem to be decorated with different pieces of art, from paintings to artifacts.
  • Illumination. Shut candles and sconces are set on the walls which can easily be lit. Otherwise, red brightstone gives a dim light where there should be total darkness.
  • Sound. The sound of a distant fireplace is heard everywhere, but the sound doesn't seem to resonate much in this otherwise empty manor.

The manor interior is easy to navigate and doesn't host many secrets. There are mainly a lot of corridors and huge rooms behind huge doors. Most of them shut, except the dining hall and a sitting room.

A few servants are found around the manor making the rounds every now and then. They all hold a special bell that will not only alarm the local garrison but also activate the statue guards all around the manor.

Vassarash the obsidian greatsword

In the sitting room, a huge obsidian blade hangs from the wall behind a glass cover filled with sigils with a small note:

DO NOT WIELD!

This beautiful obsidian blade holds a dragon. It will chop heads at the same time that it snares you into its dark goals.

Manor crypt
...

Either conducted by a servant or through thorough investigation, eventually, the group will find the old Moonfire's crypt where the cultists are hiding.

The crypt is located in the basement although it goes down for three more stories, the cultists are in the main chamber on the top floor of the crypt.

The basement continues through the other wings of the manor, although its function is mainly storage facilities for the servants to use.

The room that the cultists are in follows one of the manor wings, its 15ft foot ceiling reaching the bottom windows of the manor. Gargoyles decorate the pillars holding the crypt as huge statues represent the buried dead. Two huge statues separate this wing of the crypt from the rest.

The cultists are doing some kind of prayer. Three of them by a huge altar, two of them standing and attending whichever ritual the one at the center is performing. The remaining four are distributed throughout the room, kneeling towards the altar. An undead companion next to them.

The four kneeling cultists can easily be dealt with, and by doing so, their undead companions will fall.

Upon an encounter starting, the two attendant cultists will activate Barrier-bond runes, which will make two (wall of force) bubbles appear, encircling them and the main cultist. The two big statues at the entrance become animated as Minotaur Skeleton.

What do the cultists know?

Intelligence that may be gathered from the cultists:

  • Runecrafting was taught to them through prayers to the god of death
  • The Spine claimed to successfully have brought the god of death to our world
  • The Spine is a recent addition. They are aligned but not fully supported by the cult.
  • The Spine has contacts with the Moonfire family. They allowed them to stay and do the gatherings in this manor.
  • The god of death has changed in recent weeks. From a thoughtful teacher to someone anxious about returning to this world.
  • The last piece to returning to this world is within Emberwalk. An ancient city deep into the Ironmouth cave. Although the investigation has already been started by the Cult.

Dramatic interrogation

Elwin will try to use Zone of Truth to get the most information they can.

However, the leader of the cultists is a Banshee-level cultist. Realizing they are spilling secrets, they will start casting blood-powered runes with their own wounds. Using:

  1. Blood spike rune to hurt/kill Elwin
  2. Blood fire rune to set the manor on fire

Conclusion
...

After returning to the Astraeran army's HQs, Alasan and Qorog await the party's return at the entrance hall. They are willing to learn all intel gathered by the party and will proceed to deeply interrogate any captured cultists as the adventurers leave. But not before inviting them to a debriefing meeting the following day to formalize the end of the contract the night is fading and everyone needs some rest.
