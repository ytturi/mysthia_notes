Arriving at HQ + Attendees
...

As the adventurers proceed to finalize the end of their contract and maybe find a clue about what to do next, they find quite a group of people at the door. Mainly Green Guards that prevent most of the usual guild's businesses from happening.

The royal guards wear green-colored half-plate armor and use halberds, although some of them do carry crossbows. On top of their armor, they also carry a tabbard displaying the Thornspine crest: A wolf with thorny spines.

Alasan is currently conversing with three persons that the adventurers may not yet know: Ranardin Greensprout, Velien's governor, Haratha Blackspine, Count of the Thornspines as well as Lord Greenthorn, Duche of the Thornspine Duchy, who are part of the Naburian Royalty and Nobleman.

Ranardin Greensprout (he/him) is a human man of shoulder-length dark green hair. His skin is lightly tanned, resembling the tone of untreated oak wood.

Haratha Blackspine (She/her) is a half-eleven woman with pale skin and dark blue eyes as well as hair, resembling that of Moonelves.

Lord Greenthorn (he/him) is a mature elven man with long green braided hair barely showing any strains. His eyes, a piercing sight and intimidating at a first glance although also a charming green.

As soon as the adventurers are spotted, Alasan will cordially gesture them to approach. The adventurers are introduced to the royalty and Alasan will explain that actually they were waiting for their arrival to proceed with the debrief.

Alasan will accompany everyone toward a big meeting hall. This room is unexpectedly prepared to hold such a level of guests with all the commodities of a low noble house, from decorations to comfortable sitting facilities.

As they enter the room, they see that there are more people already gathered in the room.

[Qorog] represents a group of capable people that can help with the current situation. Although there's not much known about them, Alasan expresses his desire of collaboration.

Qorog (they/them) is a Tortle representing the The Order of the Golden Fountain

Lady Regina of Seacrest (who the Characters know as Agent Regi) is also present since it was on her instruction that the Cult of Thanatos and the Greentongue bandits were investigated. Lady Regina has additional knowledge of the situation and will debrief that during the meeting. Lady Regina is part of both The Obsidian Veil and the Naburian Royalty and Nobleman

Lady Regina of Seacrest (she/her) is a Tiefling with dark long straight horns and a black spiked tail. Her shoulder-length hair is blue-colored and decorated with several small braids and trinkets including chains linking both her horns and ears.

Although the adventurers did meet Agent Regi before, Lady Regina looks way more refined. She is wearing a dress and many trinkets which makes it slightly hard to identify her at a first glance.

They are both speaking with Lady Keishara Silverwater a headmistress of Umbral Wing Academia and Dath Kragforge, a Forgemaster of the Astraeran Academy.

Lady Keishara Silverwater (she/them) is an Avaril, a wind-touched elf. Her long white hair is braided into intrincate ornaments and her slender figure is tightly grasped by a soft-looking one-piece dress. A sophisticated half-cape is hanging from one of her shoulders, displaying the Umbral Wing's crest.

Dath Kargforge (he/him) is a light-skinned stout dwarf with several burn marks in his skin. Although they do look healed, the scars still present in his body and his lack of eyebrows or eyelids suggests he is often too close to the fire. Dath wears some piece of machinery on his left arm and a prosthetic on his right leg.

The meeting - Introduction
...

After a few moments, Alasan will ask for everyone to gather around and have a sit if they want.

He will proceed to explain the situation as known and why everyone here has been gathered.

  • The recent events in Velien are not casual. There was someone guiding such events someone for which our intelligence gathering has revealed as The Spine. The Spine, although connected to the Cult of Thanatos, is actually an external party that was trying to gain their favor for something we can only suspect of even darker intent. And as far as we know, they may have succeeded in everything they wanted from the cult. Access to an old relic said to be the prime channeler of Thanatos. According to the cult members we captured, such a relic is known only by a few within the cult. A few who actually died under suspicious circumstances a few weeks ago. This is the event we suspect triggered the ones that followed into the events we know on Velien.
  • Events that this group of adventurers has been dealing with at a lack of a proper commission. Would you mind stepping up and explaining all the events you have encountered since the raid of the Orc camp?
  • But this is not where the story ends. The cult of Thanatos was looking for something, something that the Spine wanted, and asked the cult to retrieve it for them. Information hidden in the darkest tomes of the Umbral wing, an evil that we fought long ago and we thought was gone.

The meeting - The Umbral Wing
...

At this point, Keishara will stand and continue.

  • About two weeks ago, our archives were breached. Not the regular library but the locked archives containing books and information that only the archmages of the Umbral Wing have access to. Although such texts do contain many dangerous rituals and old magic that should be kept forgotten, they do also contain knowledge that can guide us to our new future and thus we kept it, guarded, locked, and encrypted.

  • However, two weeks ago, we found dead guards and a knocked-out archivist at the doors of the archive. Although we did sound the alarm, the intruders knew what they were looking for and had means of escaping.

  • They stole three things:

    • A scroll with details of an artifact, something schemed in the last era. Something evil that combined the use of blood runes and prosthetics, using souls as a catalyst to create an artifact capable of fusing with one's soul. Sort of a prosthetic eye for which there are records of existing only once. Records that can only be found in the Umbral Wing for they did belong to the one that started the new era five-hundred years ago.
    • The second item that was stolen is a tome. This tome contains the rules to make use of a specific sort of forbidden magic: blood runes. Although a glimpse of such rules still exists in all the archives that a proper wizard could have access to, these specific ones were long forbidden for their origin comes from the hells and they entangle life and souls as a source. A knowledge once studied and refined by old alumni until the end of the last era, when the gods themselves agreed on removing such magic from our hands.
    • The third item is a diary. More like a letter than a diary. A collection of papers containing information on how to access the Emberwalk Halls. The old dwarven city that was long since forgotten and missing from any records for that is how the gods finished the second era.

  • Although most of the knowledge regarding those three items should be long gone, artifacts and dark entities may have had the power to retain such information. And it is because of that, that I went to visit the Sen Tarum oracle myself and confirmed such information.

Lady Regina of Seacrest - Obisdian Veil
...

A second before havoc starts after knowing of the missing items, Lady Regina will intervene:

  • Let's not forget the current loose ends.

    • We have cultists in a respectable house, we should check how they had access to it.
    • We have missing people in Velien, in theory, the cult still has them and plans to use them in a ritual.
    • The cultists were amassing bodies only god knows the reason for.

  • The Spine seems to be behind all of the above. So I'd suggest that we focus our efforts on them. We should send a party to visit Kaldruk Moonfire. He has not been around the city for years anyway, but a cordial visit should be in order just to rule it out.

  • Now, with what we have learned so far:

    • (Pointing towards the group of adventurers) You lot found a hideout in the mountains with stairs so deep that they seemed to reach the Underdark. That could be this Emberwalk city we should at least guard the entrance to the cave while we investigate further.
    • You also mentioned seeing some sort of prosthetic eye in a chamber on the other side of the portal that the cult created, right? That could be the eye from the stolen scroll. Keishara, is there any way of knowing where this artifact could be? We should investigate such a path and destroy the artifact before it falls into the wrong hands.

  • Is there something else that I'm missing? Do you agree with the plan then?

Lord Greenthorn - Noble houses
...

Thanks for the debrief and the suggestions. I concur that there are such paths of action. I'll employ my resources to it.

Let's further discuss follow-up plans and suggestions but at least proceed with such actions.

Alasan, could you be so kind as to send this group along with some of your choosing to pay a visit to lord Kaldruk in Velien?, I'm sure lord Ranardin could do the appropriate write-up for this situation.

Also, sending a group towards the cave to investigate and guard the entrance should be wise, so if you could take care of that as well, we'd rather have the royal guard present in case something actually goes wrong. If that is indeed the entrance to Emberwalk Halls, then master Dath will indeed be a really important asset for this quest, so he'll be summoned there to ascertain such a situation.

As per the artifact, Keishara, although I don't doubt your academia's power, you should be able to collaborate with the Astraeran academy to find out more about the location of such a dangerous artifact and deal with it, right? I'm counting on you.

For now, let us proceed with this plan. Dismissed.
